# academic-writing-clean

English-language academic writing and revision for medical manuscripts, especially original research and clinical research papers.

## What it does

`academic-writing-clean` helps turn research content into journal-ready English that is clear, specific, controlled, and less AI-like.

It is designed for:

- Titles
- Abstracts
- Introduction, Methods, Results, and Discussion
- Cover letters
- Response-to-reviewer revisions
- Section rewrites and polishing

## Best for

Use this skill when manuscript text feels:

- generic
- wordy
- repetitive
- overly abstract
- overly formal
- AI-generated in tone

## Workflow

1. Identify the manuscript type, audience, and target journal.
2. Build an outline from the research question, figures, tables, and main findings.
3. Draft Methods and Results first if needed.
4. Draft the Introduction after the contribution is clear.
5. Draft the Discussion from main finding to context, limitations, and implications.
6. Write the title and abstract last.
7. Revise for clarity, structure, and anti-AI style.

## Core principles

- Prefer concrete claims over generic claims.
- Keep one sentence to one main point whenever possible.
- Make Methods reproducible and Results factual.
- Keep the Discussion interpretive but restrained.
- Replace vague, machine-like phrasing with data, actor, action, or consequence.

## Section guidance

- **Title**: short, specific, searchable
- **Abstract**: standalone summary, written last
- **Introduction**: background, gap, contribution
- **Methods**: enough detail for reproducibility
- **Results**: report findings, not interpretation
- **Discussion**: explain meaning, limits, and implications without overclaiming

## Anti-AI check

Before finalizing, ask:

- Is this sentence concrete?
- Is it saying something useful?
- Did I remove empty formal language?
- Did I avoid stock phrases and vague hedging?
- Does it sound natural rather than templated?
